What Are Dental Veneers?

Dental veneers, also commonly called tooth veneers or cosmetic veneers, are ultra-thin shells that are bonded directly to the front surface of your natural teeth. They are custom-designed to match the color, shape and size of your surrounding teeth, resulting in a seamless smile that looks entirely natural while correcting a wide range of aesthetic concerns.

Veneers are most commonly used to address the following issues:

  • Severe tooth discoloration or stains that teeth whitening cannot fully resolve
  • Chipped, cracked or worn enamel — chip repair veneers are one of our most requested treatments
  • Gaps between teeth — gap correction with veneers is fast and highly effective
  • Slightly misaligned or uneven teeth that affect the harmony of your smile
  • Teeth that are too small or disproportionate in relation to the rest of your smile
  • Worn-down edges from grinding or age

"Porcelain veneers are often called the gold standard of cosmetic dentistry because they deliver results that are virtually indistinguishable from natural teeth while providing lasting durability and stain resistance that most other cosmetic treatments simply cannot match."

Types of Veneers We Offer at Our Clifton Dental Clinic

At Porcelain Veneers in Clifton, NJ, we offer several types of dental veneers to meet the diverse needs and budgets of our patients. Understanding the differences between these options is an important part of determining which treatment will best achieve your smile goals.

Porcelain Veneers

Porcelain veneers are the most popular and widely recommended type of dental veneers for good reason. Made from high-quality ceramic material, they are incredibly lifelike in appearance, reflecting light just as natural tooth enamel does. They are also highly durable and stain-resistant veneers, meaning your smile will stay bright even as you enjoy coffee, tea, red wine and other foods that typically discolor natural teeth.

The porcelain veneer procedure typically requires two visits to our Clifton dental clinic. During the first visit, your dentist will prepare the teeth by removing a small amount of enamel and take precise impressions that are sent to a dental laboratory. Custom veneers are fabricated to your exact specifications and bonded permanently to your teeth at the second appointment. With proper care, porcelain veneers can last anywhere from 10 to 20 years.

Ceramic Veneers

Ceramic veneers are closely related to porcelain veneers and are sometimes used interchangeably with the term. They are fabricated from advanced dental ceramics that provide excellent strength, aesthetics and biocompatibility. Ceramic veneers are an ideal choice for patients seeking natural-looking veneers that blend seamlessly with their existing teeth. They are highly resistant to chipping and offer long-term performance comparable to porcelain.

Composite Veneers

For patients who are looking for affordable veneers or prefer a less invasive option, composite veneers are an excellent alternative. Unlike porcelain, composite veneers are made from tooth-colored resin material and can often be applied directly to the teeth in a single office visit — no laboratory work required. This makes them a convenient choice for patients with busy schedules or those who want to see their results right away.

While composite veneers are generally less durable than porcelain and may require touch-ups over time, they offer the advantage of being reversible. Our team can help you weigh the pros and cons of each option during your veneer consultation at our Clifton, NJ office.

Minimal Prep Veneers

Minimal prep veneers — sometimes called no-prep veneers — are an increasingly popular option for patients who want to preserve as much of their natural tooth structure as possible. As the name suggests, these veneers require little to no removal of enamel before placement, making the procedure less invasive and, in some cases, completely reversible. Minimal prep veneers are thinner than traditional veneers and are best suited for patients with mild cosmetic concerns who want a conservative approach to veneer placement.

The Smile Makeover Experience: What to Expect

A smile transformation with veneers is a carefully planned, multi-step process. At Porcelain Veneers dental clinic in Clifton, NJ, we believe that an informed patient is a happy patient. Here is a detailed breakdown of the veneer procedure from start to finish:

Step 1 — Veneer Consultation

Every smile makeover at our Clifton dental clinic begins with a thorough veneer consultation. During this appointment, your dentist will examine your teeth, discuss your aesthetic goals and review your overall oral health. We use digital smile design tools to give you a preview of what your finished results could look like. This is also the time to ask questions, discuss your budget and explore which type of veneers — porcelain, ceramic or composite — is the best fit for your needs.

Step 2 — Tooth Preparation

For traditional porcelain and ceramic veneers, the next step is tooth preparation. Your dentist will gently remove a thin layer of enamel from the front of each tooth that will receive a veneer. This step is necessary to ensure the veneer sits flush with your surrounding teeth for a completely natural appearance. Local anesthesia is used to ensure your comfort throughout the procedure. If you are receiving minimal prep veneers, this step is significantly reduced or eliminated entirely.

Step 3 — Impressions and Temporary Veneers

Once your teeth are prepared, precise impressions are taken and sent to our dental laboratory, where your custom veneers are crafted to exact specifications. During the two-week waiting period, temporary veneers are placed so you can maintain your appearance and protect your prepared teeth. These temporaries also give you a chance to preview your new smile and provide feedback before the final veneers are made.

Step 4 — Veneer Placement

When your custom veneers are ready, you will return to our Clifton dental clinic for the final veneer placement appointment. Your dentist will check the fit, shape and color of each veneer before permanently bonding them to your teeth using a strong dental adhesive and curing light. Any final adjustments are made to ensure a perfect bite and a beautiful result. Most patients leave this appointment absolutely thrilled with their new smile.

Step 5 — Follow-Up Care

After your veneers are placed, a follow-up appointment is scheduled to check on your bite alignment and the health of your gums. We also provide personalized guidance on caring for your new smile to maximize the lifespan of your durable veneers and keep them looking their best for years to come.

Veneers vs. Teeth Whitening: Which Should You Choose?

One of the most common questions we hear at our Clifton dental clinic is whether veneers or teeth whitening is the better option for achieving a brighter smile. While professional teeth whitening is an excellent choice for patients whose only concern is staining, it has significant limitations. It cannot fix chips, cracks, gaps or misalignment. It also does not produce lasting results for patients with intrinsic staining caused by antibiotics, fluorosis or trauma.

Porcelain veneers, on the other hand, serve as a powerful teeth whitening alternative while simultaneously addressing multiple cosmetic concerns in a single treatment. The ceramic material used in veneers is inherently stain-resistant, meaning your results will last far longer than traditional whitening treatments. For patients who want a truly comprehensive smile transformation, veneers are almost always the superior long-term investment.

Gap Correction and Chip Repair with Veneers

Two of the most transformative applications of dental veneers are gap correction and chip repair. Many patients come to Porcelain Veneers in Clifton, NJ specifically because they have a noticeable gap between their front teeth — a condition known as diastema — that makes them self-conscious in photos and social situations. Veneers can close these gaps beautifully without the need for orthodontic treatment, delivering immediate visible results that look completely natural.

Chip repair veneers are equally life-changing. A chipped tooth — especially a front tooth — can dramatically affect the overall appearance of a smile and even the patient's self-confidence. Veneers cover the damaged area entirely and restore the tooth's original shape and proportions. Combined with our precise color-matching process, the result is indistinguishable from a naturally whole tooth.

Are Veneers Right for You? Ideal Candidates

Dental veneers are suitable for most adults in good overall oral health. The ideal candidate for veneers at our Clifton dental clinic is someone who:

  • Has healthy teeth and gums with no active decay or gum disease
  • Wants to address one or more cosmetic concerns such as staining, chips, gaps or unevenness
  • Has realistic expectations about the outcome of treatment
  • Is committed to maintaining good oral hygiene and attending regular dental checkups
  • Does not have severe bruxism (teeth grinding) without a protective night guard

If you have existing dental health concerns such as cavities or gum disease, these will need to be treated before veneer placement. Our team will assess your candidacy thoroughly during your veneer consultation at our Clifton, NJ location.

Caring for Your Veneers: Tips for a Lasting Smile

Proper care is essential to getting the maximum lifespan out of your custom veneers. Here are our top recommendations for patients at Porcelain Veneers dental clinic:

  • Brush twice daily with a non-abrasive fluoride toothpaste and a soft-bristled toothbrush
  • Floss daily to remove plaque from between teeth and along the gum line
  • Avoid biting hard objects such as ice, pens or hard candy that could chip your veneers
  • Wear a custom night guard if you are prone to grinding or clenching your teeth during sleep
  • Attend regular professional cleanings and checkups at our Clifton dental clinic every six months
  • Limit consumption of highly pigmented foods and beverages, especially with composite veneers

Frequently Asked Questions About Veneers

Honest answers from the team at Porcelain Veneers dental clinic in Clifton, NJ.

What are porcelain veneers?

Porcelain veneers are custom ultra-thin ceramic shells bonded to the front surface of teeth. They correct chips, stains, gaps and uneven shapes for a natural-looking smile transformation. At Porcelain Veneers in Clifton, NJ, we use high-quality materials and a precision crafting process for stunning results.

How long do dental veneers last?

With proper care, porcelain and ceramic veneers typically last between 10 and 20 years. Composite veneers generally last 5 to 7 years. Attending regular checkups at our Clifton dental clinic and following good oral hygiene practices will significantly extend the life of your veneers.

Are veneers painful?

The veneer procedure is minimally invasive and performed under local anesthesia to ensure your comfort. Most patients report little to no discomfort during or after the procedure. Any mild sensitivity following the placement typically resolves within a few days.

What is the difference between porcelain and composite veneers?

Porcelain veneers are lab-fabricated ceramic shells that offer superior durability, stain resistance and a highly natural appearance. They require two appointments. Composite veneers are made from resin applied directly to the teeth in one visit and are a more affordable option, though they are less durable over time. Our team will help you choose the right option during your veneer consultation.

How much do veneers cost in Clifton, NJ?

Veneer costs vary depending on the type of veneer, the number of teeth being treated and the complexity of your case. We offer both affordable veneers (composite) and premium porcelain options, as well as flexible payment plans. Call our Clifton clinic at 973-718-5314 for a personalized quote.
